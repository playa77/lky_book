# Outline Audit — What Would Lee Kuan Yew Tell You?

## Proposed mode: nf (nonfiction)

**Reasoning:** The outline is structured as an argument-driven nonfiction work — diagnostic, analytical, and prescriptive — advancing a thesis about Western civilizational decline, governance quality, and the AGI transition, using Lee Kuan Yew as a diagnostic/prescriptive framework. It contains chapters organized around claims, evidence, case studies, counterargument handling, and a forward argumentative arc. No fictional narrative elements or invented characters are present.

## Proposed leaf granularity: section-leaf

Each chapter is divided into numbered sections (e.g., 1.1 through 1.5) ranging 800–2,000 words each, each constituting a single coherent argumentative or expository move (one claim-cluster, one case study, one counterargument segment). Chapters total 5,000–9,000 words, too large and internally diverse for single-unit drafting. **Section-leaf** is the appropriate granularity.
