# Book Premise

## Title
**What Would Lee Kuan Yew Tell You?**
*A Dead Pragmatist's Guide to Surviving What's Coming*

## Core Thesis
The West is experiencing measurable civilizational decline by multiple metrics — institutional competence, educational outcomes, social cohesion, infrastructure quality, governance quality — at the exact moment it needs to navigate the most consequential technological transition in human history (AGI). Lee Kuan Yew, who spent fifty years building a first-world nation from nothing while watching the West squander its inheritance, offers the most useful diagnostic and prescriptive framework available — precisely because he was unsentimental, pragmatic, and had no ideological loyalty to defend.

## The Vector
The book is not a topic catalog. It moves along a single arc:

**Shock** (what Asia built while you weren't looking) → **Diagnosis** (what LKY saw breaking in the West) → **Method** (how Singapore actually solved problems) → **Accelerant** (AGI changes everything and makes governance failure existential) → **Fork** (decline or radical pragmatism — choose) → **Personal Charge** (what you do about it individually)

Every chapter pushes toward the next. The reader should feel increasing urgency, not decreasing interest.

## Audience
Primary: Western readers aged 20-45 — educated, politically aware but not ideologically captured, concerned about the future, open to uncomfortable conclusions. The person who reads The Atlantic, listens to long-form podcasts, and suspects something is seriously wrong but hasn't found a framework that doesn't come with ideological baggage.

Secondary: Policy professionals, educators, and technologists who need a framework for thinking about institutional reform in the AGI era.

## What This Book Is Not
- Not an apology for authoritarianism. LKY's methods had real costs and the book must acknowledge them honestly.
- Not Asia cheerleading. Chapter 16 (What Asia Still Gets Wrong) was in v0.1 for a reason — intellectual honesty demands it, and it's folded into the v0.2 structure where relevant.
- Not a policy white paper. It's a book-length argument written for general readers, not specialists.
- Not a biography of LKY. He's the lens, not the subject.
